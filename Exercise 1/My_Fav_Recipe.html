Aglio E Olio

Aglio E Olio is a really simple and quick pasta dish from the city of Naples, Italy.
It's famous for using simple and readily available ingredients.
This dish originated in Southern Italy and was a quick and easy meal for lower class. Dish's name is self explanatory, Aglio meaning garlic and Olio meaning oil.

Ingredients

  • Spaghetti
  • Olive Oil
  • Garlic
  • Chilli Flakes
  • Salt
  • Fresh Parsley

Method of Cooking

  1. Cook Spaghetti in salted water till its near being Al dente.
  2. Add 3 to 4 tablespoons of olive oil in a different pan and heat it over medium.
  3. Once oil is hot, add 4 to 8 sliced or minced garlic cloves depending on how garlicky you like your food.
  4. Add red chilli flakes to the taste right after garlic.
  5. saut√© till garlic is light brown/golden.
  6. Once garlic is the right color, add 1/2 cup or 100ml of past water and with that add your cooked spaghetti.
  7. Toss pasta around and cook it in the sauce until the spaghetti is finely coated in the sauce.
  8. if sauce is a bit dry, add more pasta water to your liking.
  9. add a pinc of salt and taste. If not enough, add another pinch of salt.
  10. Garnish it with chopped fresh parsley and it's ready to serve.
